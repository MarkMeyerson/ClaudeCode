## **Getting Started**

### **Step 1: Determine Your Eligibility**

The platform is designed for:
- 501(c)(3) charitable organizations
- 501(c)(4) social welfare organizations
- 501(c)(5) labor organizations
- 501(c)(6) trade and professional associations
- 527 political organizations
- PACs (connected, non-connected, Super PACs)
- Other tax-exempt non-profit organizations

### **Step 2: Gather Required Information**

Before starting the intake process, gather:

#### **Basic Organization Information:**
- Legal name and any DBAs (Doing Business As names)
- EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- Incorporation date and state
- IRS determination letter (if applicable)
- Current fiscal year end date

#### **Leadership Information:**
- Board chair contact information
- Executive Director/CEO information
- Board member list with contact details
- Key staff roster

#### **Financial Information:**
- Most recent Form 990 (if filed)
- Current fiscal year budget
- Most recent audited financial statements
- Revenue and expense breakdown

#### **Program Information:**
- Mission and vision statements
- Primary program descriptions
- Service area and beneficiaries served
- Impact data and outcomes

#### **Compliance Documents:**
- State charity registrations
- FEC filings (for PACs)
- Current licenses and certifications
- Recent audit reports

### **Step 3: Allow Adequate Time**

The intake and assessment process varies by organization:

- **Quick Intake** (Basic Information): 30-45 minutes
- **Comprehensive Intake**: 2-3 hours
- **Full Assessment**: 4-8 hours (can be completed over multiple sessions)
- **Documentation Upload**: 1-2 hours

**Best Practice**: Plan to complete the intake in 2-3 sessions rather than all at once.

## **Understanding Organization Types**

The platform uses intelligent classification to route organizations to the appropriate track.

### **Mission-Driven Organizations (Charities & Foundations)**

**Characteristics:**
- 501(c)(3) tax status
- Primary focus on charitable, educational, religious, or scientific purposes
- Typically engage in direct service, advocacy, research, or grantmaking
- Revenue from donations, grants, and program fees
- Serve beneficiaries and communities

**Examples:**
- Food banks and homeless shelters
- Educational institutions
- Healthcare organizations
- Environmental conservation groups
- Arts and cultural organizations
- Private foundations and community foundations

**Assessment Focus:**
- Program effectiveness and impact measurement
- Beneficiary outcomes
- Volunteer management
- Donor stewardship
- Community partnerships
- Evidence-based practices

### **Professional & Trade Associations**

**Characteristics:**
- 501(c)(6) tax status (typically)
- Member-driven organizations
- Serve a specific industry, profession, or trade
- Revenue primarily from membership dues and events
- Provide education, networking, and advocacy

**Examples:**
- Medical and legal professional associations
- Industry trade associations
- Chambers of commerce
- Professional societies
- Certification bodies

**Assessment Focus:**
- Member engagement and satisfaction
- Member retention and growth
- Value proposition to members
- Advocacy effectiveness
- Revenue diversification
- Industry positioning

### **Political Action Committees (PACs)**

**Characteristics:**
- 527 tax status or 501(c)(4)
- Focused on political contributions and advocacy
- Subject to FEC and/or state election commission regulations
- Revenue from individual donors, PAC contributions
- Support candidates or issue advocacy

**Examples:**
- Connected PACs (affiliated with corporations or unions)
- Non-connected PACs (independent)
- Super PACs (independent expenditure-only)
- Leadership PACs
- Hybrid PACs

**Assessment Focus:**
- Donor acquisition and retention
- Campaign effectiveness
- Political strategy and targeting
- Compliance with election law
- Grassroots mobilization
- Issue advocacy impact

### **How Classification Works**

The platform uses multiple signals to classify your organization:

1. **Tax Status**: Your IRS classification (501(c)(3), 501(c)(6), 527, etc.)
2. **Mission Analysis**: Natural language processing of your mission statement
3. **Program Activities**: Analysis of primary programs and services
4. **Revenue Sources**: Breakdown of income streams
5. **Organizational Structure**: Membership model, board structure, etc.

**AI-Assisted Classification**: Our machine learning model analyzes these inputs to suggest the best track with confidence scores. You can always override the suggestion if needed.

## **The Intake Process**

### **Intake Stages**

The intake process consists of five stages:

1. **Initial** - Basic information collection
2. **In Progress** - Comprehensive data gathering
3. **Review** - System and staff review of submitted data
4. **Approved** - Ready for assessment
5. **Onboarding** - Guided setup and training

### **Step-by-Step Intake Guide**

#### **Stage 1: Basic Organization Information**

**Time Required**: 15-20 minutes

**Information Needed**:
- Organization name (legal and DBA)
- EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- Tax status (501(c)(3), 501(c)(6), 527, etc.)
- Incorporation state and date
- Fiscal year end
- Contact information (address, phone, email, website)

**Pro Tips**:
- Have your IRS determination letter handy for exact dates
- Enter your EIN without dashes (e.g., 123456789 not 12-3456789)
- Ensure your fiscal year end is in MM-DD format (e.g., 12-31 for calendar year)

#### **Stage 2: Leadership & Governance**

**Time Required**: 20-30 minutes

**Information Needed**:
- Executive Director/CEO information
- Board Chair information
- Number of board members
- Board member list (names, titles, contact info)
- Key staff roster
- Committee structure

**Pro Tips**:
- Prepare a current board roster before starting
- Include both work and personal contact information where appropriate
- Note any board vacancies or upcoming term expirations

#### **Stage 3: Financial Information**

**Time Required**: 30-45 minutes

**Information Needed**:
- Annual budget
- Total revenue and sources
- Total expenses and breakdown
- Total assets and liabilities
- Net assets
- Program/admin/fundraising expense ratios
- Reserve levels
- Major funders

**Pro Tips**:
- Use your most recent Form 990 as a reference
- Have your latest financial statements available
- Be prepared to explain any unusual financial situations
- Calculate months of operating reserves in advance

#### **Stage 4: Programs & Services**

**Time Required**: 30-60 minutes

**Information Needed**:
- Mission statement
- Vision statement (if different from mission)
- Core values
- Primary programs and descriptions
- Beneficiaries served (demographics and numbers)
- Geographic service area
- Outcomes and impact data
- Partnerships and collaborations

**Pro Tips**:
- Keep program descriptions concise but specific
- Quantify impact where possible (e.g., "served 500 families" not "served many families")
- Highlight evidence-based or innovative approaches
- Mention any program evaluations or assessments completed

#### **Stage 5: Compliance & Operations**

**Time Required**: 20-30 minutes

**Information Needed**:
- Form 990 filing status
- State charity registrations
- FEC registrations (for PACs)
- Audit history
- Insurance coverage
- Licenses and certifications
- Technology systems used
- Risk management practices

**Pro Tips**:
- List all states where you're registered to solicit donations
- Note any compliance issues or corrective actions taken
- Include both required and voluntary certifications
- Describe your main technology systems (accounting, CRM, etc.)

#### **Stage 6: Document Upload**

**Time Required**: 30-60 minutes

**Required Documents** (varies by organization type):

**All Organizations**:
- IRS Determination Letter
- Most recent Form 990
- Current board roster
- Articles of Incorporation
- Bylaws
- Conflict of Interest Policy

**Mission-Driven Organizations**:
- Annual Report
- Audited Financial Statements
- Program evaluations
- Strategic Plan

**Associations**:
- Membership agreement/contract
- Member survey results
- Conference attendance records
- Advocacy wins documentation

**PACs**:
- FEC Form 1 (Statement of Organization)
- Most recent FEC reports
- State PAC registrations
- Donor management reports

**Document Guidelines**:
- Accepted formats: PDF, DOC, DOCX, XLS, XLSX, JPG, PNG
- Maximum file size: 50 MB per file
- Use clear, descriptive file names
- Ensure documents are current (within past 2 years)
- Remove any personally identifiable information (PII) unless required

### **Auto-Save & Progress Tracking**

The platform automatically saves your progress every 30 seconds. You can:
- Exit at any time and resume later
- See completion percentage in real-time
- Review and edit previously entered information
- Track which sections are complete vs. pending

### **Data Verification**

As you enter information, the platform performs real-time verification:

✓ **EIN Verification**: Checks format and validates against IRS database (if available)

✓ **Email Validation**: Ensures valid email format

✓ **URL Validation**: Checks website URL format

✓ **Cross-Field Validation**: Ensures financial data adds up correctly

✓ **Completeness Check**: Alerts you to missing required information

⚠️ **Duplicate Detection**: Alerts if another organization with similar information exists

### **Public Data Enrichment**

If you provide an EIN, the platform can automatically pull data from public sources:

**IRS Exempt Organizations Database**:
- Tax status confirmation
- Subsection code
- Determination letter date
- Group exemption number

**GuideStar/Candid**:
- Mission statement
- Program areas
- Financial data
- Leadership information

**Charity Navigator**:
- Overall rating
- Financial health score
- Accountability & transparency score

**FEC Database** (for PACs):
- Committee ID
- Committee type
- Treasurer information
- Recent filings

You can review and accept/reject any auto-populated data.

## **Assessment Overview**

After completing intake, you'll begin the comprehensive assessment process.

### **Assessment Dimensions**

The assessment evaluates your organization across multiple dimensions:

#### **1. Organizational Capacity** (Weight: 15%)
- Strategic planning maturity
- Decision-making processes
- Organizational structure
- Change management
- Leadership development

#### **2. Financial Health** (Weight: 15%)
- Revenue sustainability
- Expense management
- Reserve adequacy
- Financial controls
- Reporting and transparency

#### **3. Governance** (Weight: 15%)
- Board effectiveness
- Fiduciary oversight
- Policy framework
- Ethics and accountability
- Stakeholder engagement

#### **4. Program Effectiveness** (Weight: 15%)
*Mission-Driven Organizations*
- Program design and quality
- Outcomes measurement
- Evidence base
- Service delivery efficiency
- Beneficiary satisfaction

#### **5. Compliance** (Weight: 10%)
- Tax compliance
- Regulatory filings
- State registrations
- Audit readiness
- Legal requirements

#### **6. Technology** (Weight: 10%)
- Technology infrastructure
- Data management
- Cybersecurity
- Digital presence
- Technology adoption

#### **7. Fundraising** (Weight: 10%)
- Fundraising strategy
- Donor management
- Revenue diversification
- Fundraising efficiency
- Major gifts program

#### **8. Human Resources** (Weight: 5%)
- Talent management
- Staff development
- Compensation and benefits
- Performance management
- Culture and retention

#### **9. Risk Management** (Weight: 5%)
- Risk identification
- Mitigation strategies
- Insurance coverage
- Crisis preparedness
- Incident response

#### **10. Impact Measurement** (Weight: 5%)
*Mission-Driven Organizations*
- Outcomes tracking
- Impact evaluation
- Data collection
- Reporting to stakeholders
- Continuous improvement

### **Track-Specific Dimensions**

**Association Track Adds**:
- Member Engagement (15%)
- Advocacy Effectiveness (10%)

**PAC Track Adds**:
- Donor Engagement (15%)
- Political Strategy (10%)
- Campaign Effectiveness (10%)

### **Assessment Format**

**Question Types**:
- **Scale Questions**: Rate on a 1-5 or 0-10 scale
- **Multiple Choice**: Select the best answer
- **Yes/No**: Binary questions
- **Text**: Short narrative responses
- **File Upload**: Supporting documentation
- **Matrix**: Multiple sub-questions in a table format

**Example Questions**:

*Financial Health*:
> "What are your organization's current months of operating reserves?"
> [Slider: 0-12+ months]

*Governance*:
> "How often does your full board meet?"
> ○ Monthly
> ○ Bi-monthly
> ○ Quarterly
> ○ Semi-annually
> ○ Annually
> ○ As needed

*Program Effectiveness*:
> "Does your organization use evidence-based or evidence-informed practices?"
> ○ Yes, rigorously evidence-based
> ○ Yes, evidence-informed
> ○ Some programs use evidence
> ○ No, but exploring
> ○ No

### **Scoring & Benchmarking**

**Scoring System**:
- Each dimension scored 0-100
- Overall score is weighted average
- Maturity levels assigned (1-5 scale)
- Percentile ranking vs. peers

**Score Interpretation**:
- **90-100**: Excellent - Exemplary practices
- **80-89**: Very Good - Strong performance
- **70-79**: Good - Solid foundation
- **60-69**: Fair - Room for improvement
- **50-59**: Needs Improvement - Significant gaps
- **Below 50**: Critical - Immediate action required

**Benchmarking**:
Your scores are compared to:
- Organizations of similar size (budget)
- Same organization type
- Same geographic region
- Same program areas (mission-driven)
- Same industry sector (associations)

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **General**

**Q: How long does the intake process take?**
A: The basic intake takes 30-45 minutes, while comprehensive intake with document upload takes 2-3 hours. You can save progress and complete over multiple sessions.

**Q: Can multiple people from my organization use the platform?**
A: Yes! You can invite unlimited team members with different permission levels (Admin, Staff, Board Member, Consultant, Viewer).

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Yes. We use bank-level encryption (256-bit SSL), secure cloud storage, and comply with SOC 2 Type II standards. We never sell or share your data.

**Q: How much does it cost?**
A: [Pricing information based on your business model]

### **Intake**

**Q: What if I don't have all the required information?**
A: You can skip optional fields and come back later. However, required fields must be completed to advance to the next stage.

**Q: Can I edit information after submitting?**
A: Yes, you can edit your intake information at any time. Changes are tracked in the audit log.

**Q: What if my organization type is incorrectly classified?**
A: You can manually override the AI classification. Go to Settings > Organization Profile > Organization Type.

### **Assessment**

**Q: How often should we complete an assessment?**
A: We recommend annual comprehensive assessments, with mini-assessments quarterly to track progress.

**Q: Can we skip questions?**
A: You can skip optional questions, but skipping will affect your completeness percentage and may impact scoring accuracy.

**Q: How long are assessment results valid?**
A: Assessment results are most useful within 12 months. After that, we recommend a new assessment to capture changes.

### **Compliance**

**Q: Does the platform actually file compliance reports for us?**
A: No, the platform tracks deadlines and requirements but does not file on your behalf. You remain responsible for all filings.

**Q: What if we miss a compliance deadline?**
A: The platform will alert you, but you must take corrective action. Consult with legal counsel if needed.

### **Technical**

**Q: What browsers are supported?**
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest versions). Internet Explorer is not supported.

**Q: Can I access the platform on mobile?**
A: Yes, the platform is mobile-responsive. A native mobile app is coming soon.

**Q: What if I forget my password?**
A: Click "Forgot Password" on the login page. You'll receive a reset link via email.
